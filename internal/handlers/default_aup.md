# End User Acceptable Use Policy

The operators of the Software Assurance Marketplace (SWAMP) have legal obligations to protect shared services, resources, and the broader cybersecurity community. You share this responsibility by observing and upholding the rules of acceptable use outlined in this document.

**"SWAMP Services"** include, but are not limited to:

- Web or API access to the SWAMP platform and its assessment tools.
- The ability to upload software code, binaries, or artifacts for security analysis.
- The ability to view, download, and manage vulnerability assessment results and reports.

Your online assent to this Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is your acknowledgment that you have read, understand, and accept your responsibilities when using SWAMP Services. If you have questions, please contact [chtc@cs.wisc.edu](mailto:chtc@cs.wisc.edu).

Violations of this AUP may result in the immediate loss of access to services. Activities in violation of any laws will be reported to the proper authorities for investigation and prosecution.

**By using SWAMP Services, you agree to comply with the following conditions of use:**

## 1. Purpose of Use

The services may only be used for the purpose of software protection, software assurance, research, or education. SWAMP is strictly designed to help identify and remediate vulnerabilities to improve software security.

## 2. Prohibited Malicious Activity

You agree not to use SWAMP Services for any malicious purposes. This includes, but is not limited to:

- Using the service to analyze malware, ransomware, or other malicious code to evade detection.
- Scanning or analyzing software, platforms, or codebases for which you do not have explicit authorization or ownership.
- Weaponizing or exploiting vulnerabilities discovered via SWAMP against any target.
- Attempting to breach, disrupt, or circumvent any of SWAMP's own administrative or security controls, or the infrastructure of its hosting providers.

## 3. Data Restrictions and Privacy

SWAMP Services do not provide the security requirements for handling or processing HIPAA, PII, CUI, FERPA, or any other data protected by statute or strict data-use agreements. You agree not to introduce these types of data (including hardcoded secrets, production database dumps, or sensitive user data embedded in code) to SWAMP Services. You are responsible for compliance with all data use agreements and copyright laws for any software or data you upload.

## 4. No Guarantees and Best-Effort Service

You understand that SWAMP is provided as a free, courtesy service. The operators do not guarantee that resources and services will be continuously available, that they will suit every purpose, or that data will never be lost or corrupted. Use of SWAMP Services is entirely at your own risk. Furthermore, SWAMP does not guarantee the accuracy, completeness, or reliability of any vulnerability scanning results.

## 5. Data Persistence and Ownership

SWAMP is not responsible for data persistence. While best efforts are made to avoid data loss, the services may incur data loss due to operator error, cybersecurity events, or routine system maintenance. You are responsible for keeping primary copies of your software and backing up your assessment results. Service operators have no liability if uploaded code or assessment data is exposed due to negligence, software bugs, misconfigurations, or malicious activity. Service operators make no claim to ownership of any code uploaded or assessment outputs produced using SWAMP.

## 6. Credential Management

You are responsible for securely managing your login credentials. This includes not sharing your login credentials with any other person, including anyone representing SWAMP services.

## 7. Prohibited Commercial and Unlawful Use

You agree not to use SWAMP Services for any of the following:

- Direct financial gain or commercial resale of the SWAMP assessment services.
- Any unlawful purpose.
- Cryptomining.
- Violation of any agreement or terms-and-conditions of software or data introduced to the service.

## 8. Legal and Export Compliance

You agree to comply with all applicable laws and relevant regulations, including United States export control laws, regarding the software and cryptographic technologies you upload or analyze.

## 9. Incident Reporting

You agree to immediately report any known or suspected security breach, unauthorized exposure of data, or misuse of your access to [chtc@cs.wisc.edu](mailto:chtc@cs.wisc.edu).

## 10. Logging and Privacy

Logged information, including information provided by you for registration purposes, is used internally for administrative, operational, accounting, monitoring, and security purposes. Following our privacy policies, this data is only used for the purposes for which it was collected unless the service operator reasonably considers that it is needed for another related reason compatible with the original purpose. You authorize the collection and sharing of this data for the purposes described above.

## 11. Right to Suspend Access

There is no guarantee, implicit or explicit, of access to the service. As part of a security investigation, an AUP violation investigation, or to preserve operational stability, service operators reserve the right to suspend or terminate your access at any time, without prior notice.

## 12. Acknowledgement of Support

If you use SWAMP Services for academic or published research, you shall provide appropriate acknowledgement of support or citation for your use of the resources as requested by the service operators.
